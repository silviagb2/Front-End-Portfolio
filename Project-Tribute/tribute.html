Quincy Jones

One of the most accomplished figures in modern music, crossing jazz, R&B, film music, and pop over a seven-decade career entailing 27 Grammy awards.

Here's a time line of Quincy Jones's life:

  • 1933 - Born in Chicago, Illinois on March 14.
  • 1950 - Win a scholarship to Boston-based music school Schillinger House.
  • 1951- Relocated to New York City, where he found work as an arranger, writing charts for Count Basie, Cannonball Adderley, Tommy Dorsey, and Dinah Washington, among others.
  • 1953 - Scored his first big break as a performer when he was added to the brass section of Lionel Hampton's orchestra.
  • 1956 - released his first album under his own name that year "This Is How I Feel About Jazz".
  • 1957 - Moved to Paris in order to study with Nadia Boulanger, an expatriate American composer.
  • 1961 - Was named a vice-president of Mercury, the first time an African-American had been hired as an upper-level executive by a major U.S. recording company
  • 1964 - Work with Count Basie led him to arrange and conduct sessions for Frank Sinatra's album "It Might as Well Be Swing."
  • 1974 - Suffered a life-threatening brain aneurysm, and while he made a full recovery, he also made a decision to cut back on his schedule to spend more time with his family.
  • 1979 - Produced Michael Jackson's breakout solo album, "Off the Wall".
  • 1982 - Jones and Jackson teamed up again for "Thriller", which went on to become the biggest-selling album of all time.
  • 1987 - Produced the benefit single "We Are the World" (written by Jackson and Lionel Richie).
  • 1985 - Produces his first screen project, the screen adaptation of Alice Walker's novel "The Color Purple", directed by Steven Spielberg.
  • 1991 - Moved into television production with the situation comedy The Fresh Prince of Bel Air, which gave Will Smith his first starring role.
  • '90s and '20s - Concentrating on his music publishing business, completing Q: The Autobiography of Quincy Jones, and being an "unofficial" cultural ambassador for the United States.
